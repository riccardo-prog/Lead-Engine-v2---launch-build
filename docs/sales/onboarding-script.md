# Onboarding Script — What To Say To Him

## Start with the problem, not the product

"Here's what happens to most realtors running ads right now. They spend money on Facebook or Google, leads come in, and then... nothing happens for hours. Sometimes the next day. By that time the lead already talked to someone else or went cold. The ones that do get followed up with, it's usually one text and then they forget. There's no system."

"The data on this is brutal — if you don't respond to a lead in the first 5 minutes, your chances of booking them drop by like 80%. Most agents are responding in 6-12 hours."

## Then explain what we built

"So we built a system that handles that entire gap. Lead comes in from an ad, a form, Instagram DM, whatever — it gets an AI response in under 2 minutes, 24/7. The AI qualifies them, asks the right questions based on what kind of lead they are, and pushes them toward booking a call. The agent doesn't have to touch anything until there's a booked appointment on their calendar."

"It's not a chatbot. It actually follows a real sales process — different scripts for buyers, sellers, investors. It knows when to push, when to back off, when to escalate to the agent."

"And the leads that don't book right away? They don't just disappear. The system keeps following up over time — scheduled check-ins, re-engagement when the timing is better. Most agents lose these people completely. We stay on them until they book or say no."

## What he's selling

"The offer is simple: we set up the entire lead conversion backend for their business. They keep running their ads, we make sure every single lead gets handled instantly, gets pushed toward a booking, and the ones that don't book get nurtured automatically until they do."

"Right now the call is a free audit — we look at how they're handling leads, show them the gaps, and if it's a fit we onboard them."

## What he should NOT say

- Don't pitch the technology. Don't say "AI" unless they ask. Say "automated system" or "we handle it."
- The nurture system is part of the offer. If they ask for specifics on how it works, say "the system follows up on a schedule — it stays on leads that didn't book and re-engages them when the timing's better. You don't have to think about it."
- Don't make up numbers. If he doesn't know, say "I'll check with Riccardo."
- Don't sell to people who aren't running ads or getting leads. The system needs leads coming in to be useful.

## How he should use his network

Don't pitch his mom or brother. Instead:

"Hey mom — who do you know that's spending on ads but frustrated because leads keep going cold? I'm working with something that fixes that."

"Hey [brother] — the agents you shoot for, are any of them complaining about lead follow-up? I might have something for them."

The goal is warm intros where the problem already exists. He shows up as a referral, not a cold seller.

## His one-liner (for texting, casual convos)

"If you're running ads, we make sure every lead gets a response in under 2 minutes, gets qualified, and gets pushed toward a booking — even at midnight. The ones that don't book get followed up with automatically. You don't touch anything until there's an appointment on your calendar."

## Pricing

**Founding rate (first 3 clients):** $2,000 setup + $1,000/mo
**Standard rate (after that):** $5,500 setup + $2,000/mo

**How to present it:** Always lead with $5,500 + $2,000/mo. If they push back on price, THEN offer the founding rate as a concession:

"Look, we're still early and building out case studies. If you're willing to be one of our first few clients and give us a testimonial once you see results, I can do $2,000 setup and $1,000 a month. That rate locks in — once we're full on founding spots, it goes to the standard price."

This way the $2,000 feels like a win for them, not a starting price. Never open with the founding rate.

Don't lead with pricing at all. Price comes up after the audit call, once they've seen the gaps in their own follow-up. If he's asked before the audit, say: "It depends on the setup — let's do the audit first so I can give you an honest answer on whether it's even worth it for your situation."

## His commission

**20% of setup fee + 10% of monthly recurring.**

- Founding rate: $400 per close + $100/mo per active client
- Standard rate: $1,100 per close + $200/mo per active client

Recurring commission pays out as long as the client stays active. If they churn, it stops. This keeps him focused on finding good-fit clients, not just anyone.

After 5 founding clients he's making $500/mo passively on top of the close bonuses. After 10 clients at standard rate, that's $2,000/mo recurring. It stacks.

## Handling price pushback

If they say it's too much, don't drop the price. Reframe the cost against what they're already losing.

**"That's a lot of money"**
"How much are you spending on ads right now? $2-3k a month? If even 30% of those leads aren't getting followed up with fast enough, you're already burning $600-900 a month on leads that go nowhere. This pays for itself if it books you one extra deal."

**"I need to think about it"**
"Totally fair. While you're thinking about it, your leads are still coming in the same way they are now. How many came in last weekend that didn't get a reply until Monday?"

**"Can you do it cheaper?"**
"The price is what it is because the system actually works — it's not a template or a chatbot. We custom-build the entire follow-up process for your business. The founding rate is already the lowest we'll ever offer this at."

**"I already have a VA / ISA doing this"**
"How fast are they responding at 10pm on a Saturday? What happens when they're sick or quit? This runs 24/7, never misses a lead, and doesn't need training. Most agents who switch to this were paying their VA more than this costs."

**"I don't get enough leads for this"**
Then they're not a fit right now. Don't force it. Say: "Makes sense — this really kicks in when you're running volume. When you start scaling your ads, hit me up and we'll get you set up."

**"Can I try it first?"**
"The audit is the trial — we'll show you exactly how your leads are being handled today and what the system would change. If it doesn't make sense after that, no hard feelings."

**General rule:** Never apologize for the price. Never negotiate on the spot. If they can't afford it, they're not the right client right now. The worst thing he can do is discount it to close someone who doesn't value it — they'll churn in 2 months anyway.

## Qualifying questions he should ask

Before booking someone for an audit, he should know:

1. Are they running ads or getting inbound leads? (If no, not a fit right now)
2. How are they handling follow-up today? (Manually? VA? Nothing?)
3. How fast are they responding? (This surfaces the pain)
4. Are they losing leads they know they should have closed? (Gets them to admit the problem)

If the answer to #1 is yes and #2 is anything other than "we have a great system," they're a fit.

## Tracking

Keep it simple. For each person he talks to, track:
- Name
- How he knows them
- Are they running ads? (Y/N)
- Current follow-up situation
- Status: Intro / Audit booked / Closed / Not a fit
- Notes
